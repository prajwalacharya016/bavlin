Partnership Proposal for Entrepreneurs

Bavlin Holdings — Outline of structure and engagement

This document is not legally binding. It is intended solely as a guideline to outline our typical structure and approach. Any partnership would be subject to formal documentation and legally binding agreements agreed by both parties.

This document outlines the standard structure and level of engagement that Bavlin proposes when entering into a partnership with entrepreneurs. It is intended as a clear point of departure for discussions and may be adapted to the specific needs of each venture.

1. Ownership structure

Under the proposed structure, Bavlin would hold a majority stake in the company, with the entrepreneur(s) being provided with equity in the venture. This reflects Bavlin’s role as the sole capital provider and strategic partner, while ensuring that founding leadership retains meaningful ownership and alignment with the long-term success of the business.

No financial investment is required from the entrepreneur(s). Bavlin provides all capital required to establish and operate the venture. Equity for the entrepreneur(s) is allocated as part of the partnership structure.

2. Role and involvement of Bavlin

Bavlin does not operate as a passive or hands-off investor. We are an active partner in the businesses we back. Our involvement includes:

  • Daily operations and management: We participate in day-to-day operational oversight and decision-making, working alongside the entrepreneur(s) to execute strategy, manage resources, and deliver results.
  • Investing and capital deployment: We are responsible for all investment and funding decisions, ensuring the company has the capital and structure needed to grow in a disciplined manner.
  • Governance and strategy: We contribute to strategic direction, governance, and key commercial and operational choices, with the aim of building a sustainable and scalable business.

This model is designed for founders who want a committed, hands-on partner—not only capital but also operational and strategic support—while retaining a significant stake and a central role in the venture.

3. No investment required from entrepreneurs

Entrepreneurs are not required to contribute capital to the company as a condition of the partnership. Bavlin provides the investment required to fund the venture; the entrepreneur(s)’ contribution is their commitment, expertise, and leadership. Equity is provided to the entrepreneur(s) to reflect that contribution and to align incentives over the long term.

4. Next steps

This proposal is a summary of our typical structure and approach. Specific terms—including governance rights, vesting, and operational roles—would be agreed in good faith and documented in formal agreements after mutual interest is confirmed. We welcome a conversation to discuss your venture and how this structure might work for you.
